Don't want to see what organ donation is?
If yes, then here's the answer:


Organ donation is the procedure in which a person allows an organ of their own to be removed and transplanted to a patient or person in need, legally, either by consent while the donor is alive or dead with the approval of the next of kin.
Donation may be done for research or, more commonly, for healthy transplantable organs and tissues may be donated to be transplanted into other person.


In confusion that is it safe or not for receiver?


For the organ receiver, the risk of transplant surgery is generally low because it is a potentially lifesaving procedure. But for donor, organ donation can expose a healthy person to the risk of and recovery from unnecessary major surgery.



What about the Donor? Is it safe for Donor?Who can Donate?


Everyone is considered to be a potential organ, eye, and tissue donor.
The age, race, ethnicity, religion, or your health (in most cases) don't matter.
If you are wondering about that if there's any age limit to donate, there's no age limit to donation or to signing up. People in their 50s, 60s, 70s, and older have donated and have been receipent of organs.
